Benthota Turtle Hatchery

All tourists like visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project. The gorgeous golden sands that entice you to our island also serve as an alluring breeding place for a number of endangered turtle species, including the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles. The research tries to preserve and safeguard the eggs from predators and other threats. Learn more about these beautiful but endangered sea creatures by visiting the turtle hatchery in Bentota.

Additionally, Bentota Beach offers the turtle hatchery project, which allows the turtles to hatch, grow, and then be released into the wild. Almost 500 endangered turtle species have been residing on the section of beach for some time. Nature and wildlife enthusiasts will undoubtedly find this to be interesting.
